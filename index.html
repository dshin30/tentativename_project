NYC PM2.5 & Traffic Volume Analysis (2016–2025)

A data-driven story about air quality, traffic behavior, and wildfire impacts.

New York City Overview

New York City is one of the most densely populated regions in the world. With millions of residents and heavy daily traffic, air quality plays a major role in public health.

Figure 1. The five boroughs of New York City.

What is PM2.5?

PM2.5 refers to particulate matter smaller than 2.5 micrometers. These particles can travel deep into the lungs and bloodstream, posing major health risks.

Figure 2. PM10, PM2.5, and PM1 entering respiratory pathways.

Traffic Conditions in NYC

NYC traffic congestion contributes significantly to daily PM2.5 levels—especially during rush hours.

Figure 3. Heavy traffic commonly seen in Manhattan.

Wildfire Smoke Impact

In 2023, large-scale wildfires in Canada sent smoke drifting across the East Coast, causing some of the worst PM2.5 readings in NYC history.
